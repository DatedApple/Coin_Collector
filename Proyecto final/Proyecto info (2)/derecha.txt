	<h2><b>The Law of Sines</b></h2>

	while cos(θ) is the ratio of the adjacent side to the hypotenuse.

<p>
	<h2> The Law of Cosines </h2> 
	In Trigonometry, the law of Cosines, also known as the Cosine Rule or Cosine Formula, basically 
	relates the length of the triangle to the cosines of one of its angles. It states that, if the length of 
	two sides and the angle between them is known for a triangle, then we can determine the length
 	of the third side. It is given by:
 
	<i>c² = a² + b² – 2ab cos a</i>
</p>

		<h2> Law of Cosine Problems </h2>
	By using cosines law,
	<i>a2 = b2 + c2 – 2bc cos(x)</i>
	Or
	<i>cos x = (b2 + c2 – a2)/2bc</i>
	We can substitute the value of the variables with the sides of the triangle, for example:
	<br>
	<i>cos(x) = (7^2 + 5^2 – 10^2)/(2 × 7 × 5)</i><br>
	<i>cos(x)=(49 + 25 -100)/70</i><br>
	<i>cos(x)= -0.37</i><br>
